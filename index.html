Cookies are text files with small pieces
of data — like a username and
password — that are used identify
your computer as you use a network.
Specific cookies are used to identify
specific users and improve their web
browsing experience. Data stored in a
cookie is created by the server
upon your connection. When the cookie is
exchanged between your computer
and the server, the server reads the ID
and knows what information to
specifically serve you. 		Cookies are in itself not harmful, but if
an attacker gets access to the files
they can steal personal information
and login to your account. In some
cases, attackers even use cookies to
send instructions or commands to website backdoors.

walking into a jewellery store to
"transport" (in this case "steal") valuables
 You should not allow cookies from

Types of Cookies

Here are the different types of cookies

Session Cookie Persistent Cookie Supercookie First-party and Third-party Cookies
Session cookies are also known as
transient cookies or per-session
cookies. Session cookies store
information while the user is visiting
the website. These cookies are
deleted once the user closes the
session. 		Persistent cookies are stored for a
specific length of time. These cookies
remain on your device until they
expire or are deleted. Persistent
cookies are sometimes called tracking
cookies because they are used to
collect user information such as
browsing habits and preferences.
 Supercookies are similar to session
cookies. However, they also have
the ability to re-create user profiles,
even after regular cookies have been
deleted. Supercookies are also stored
in different places than standard
cookies. Supercookies are sometimes
called "zombie cookies" or
"evercookies."
 First-party cookies are cookies set by
websites that users directly visit.
These cookies often store information
that is relevant or related to the site,
such as preferred settings or user
location.

Third-party cookies are cookies that
come alongside third-party content,
such as embedded videos, ads, web
banners, and scripts, on a visited
website that users visit. Advertisers
often use third-party cookies to track
user behavior.
